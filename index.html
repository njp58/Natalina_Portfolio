DEBUT: Block Phantom Limb Pain

Working on DEBUT, my project team, we developed a responsive embedded system that detects the motion phase of an amputated limb during walking and delivers targeted electrical stimulation through a TENS unit at key pain-triggering moments to combat phantom limb pain. Using a gyroscope to pinpoint the high-load phase, the device continuously tracks the limb's motion cycle. When the limb reaches the midpoint of the walking cycle, a gentle electrical pulse is delivered via the TENS device to interfere with the pain. This stimulation acts as a neural distration, reducing the perception of pain. For more information, please click on the image.

Leadership

Cornell Engineering Leadership Certification Program

During my senior year, I was admitted into the Cornell Engineering Leadership Certificaiton Program. This year-long program is designed to teach engineering students fundamental leadership skills through interactive workshops and self-disocvery. During this program, my group and I implemented a semester-long Mastermind Circles Program. As leaders of the program, we designed weekly meetings to aid participants in learning valuable tools and achieving their respective goals. The mission of this program was to aid students in achieving their goals by working together to ideate new methods and maintain accountability for achieving our individualized goals.

DEBUT Subteam Lead

For 3 semesters, I was a subteam ledd for DEBUT, a Cornell Engineering project team dedicated to designing novel biomedical devices targeted towards underrepresented communities. I planned meeting agendas, led productive meetings, and helped manage the team's progress throughout our ideation and initial prototyping phases. Under my leadership, my team and I designed a self-automated postpartum hemorrhage device that would vary in applied abdominal pressure according to live readings of blood loss recorded by a flow rate sensor. I aided in the design process and implementation of this embedded system.
